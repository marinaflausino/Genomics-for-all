Pharmacogenomics

A major part of Precision Medicine is Pharmacogenomics—the study of how your unique genes affect your body's response to medications.

Instead of the traditional "one-size-fits-all" approach, doctors can use your genetic blueprint to predict which drug and dosage will be safest and most effective for you from day one. This drastically reduces adverse drug reactions and speeds up recovery times.

🩸

Cardiovascular & Hematological Conditions

Genomic medicine is heavily reshaping hematology. A critical area of research and clinical application is Thrombophilia—a condition where the blood has an increased tendency to form dangerous clots (Venous Thromboembolism).

By screening for specific genetic variations, such as the Factor V Leiden mutation or the Prothrombin (G20210A) mutation, healthcare providers can accurately assess a patient's inherited risk. This insight allows for early, life-saving preventative strategies, tailored anticoagulation therapy, and safer surgical planning.
