Understanding Genomics

Moving beyond single genes to understand the complete instruction manual of the human body.

The Blueprint of Life

What exactly is a Genome?

Think of your genome as an entire instruction manual for your body. It contains all the DNA needed to build and maintain you. By reading this complete manual, doctors can better understand how to protect your health and treat diseases with incredible precision.

Genetics vs. Genomics

While they sound similar, their scope and application in modern healthcare are vastly different.

🔬

Traditional Genetics

Traditional genetics looks at single genes and how traits or diseases are passed down from generation to generation.

  • Focus: A single gene or a small group of genes.
  • Scale: Narrow and targeted.
  • Use Case: Testing for inherited conditions like Cystic Fibrosis or Sickle Cell.
🧬

Modern Genomics

Genomics studies all of a person’s genes (the genome) and how they interact with each other and the environment.

  • Focus: The entire interconnected DNA sequence.
  • Scale: Massive and comprehensive.
  • Use Case: Precision medicine, whole genome sequencing (WGS), and advanced cancer profiling.
